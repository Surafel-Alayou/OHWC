Our logo

Our logo is fun and colourful, the children playing give movement to the image, the hands coming together represent unity in the shape of a heart. The children are all different colours and ethnicities all with different abilities representing the diversity at The One Heart Wholeness Centre.

Our long term vision

The One Heart Wholeness Centre serves as the inaugural step toward our broader vision: KYAAH village, an inclusive haven for a disenfranchised community seeking to rebuild their lives. Our ultimate goal is to create a place where marginalized elders, abandoned children unfairly labeled as 'street boys,' and single mothers who fell prey to predators during vulnerable moments can find refuge, support, and opportunity.

KYAAH village will encompass a diverse array of residential homes, including spaces for the elderly, abandoned children, single mothers, and disabled children with high dependency needs. Additionally, it will house educational and therapeutic facilities, a health clinic, and administrative offices. The central longhouse will promote community and bonding, fostering a sense of belonging and family among its inhabitants. In time, this village will become a cherished and enduring home for all who call it their own.

Our house

The OHWC currently rents a large house on the banks of the River Nile on Bezawit Road which is the road leading to the summer palace of H.I.M. Emperor Haille Selassie. The road is lined with amazing Jakaranda trees with incredible purple blossom throughout most of the year. The house is in a peaceful position surrounded by nature and natural wildlife; its not unusual to see a hippo swim past when standing on the front balcony.

Our current vehicle

Currently we have a 16 seater mini-bus which we use to escort the children and staff to and from the project. We have 16 children on the bus together with 9 staff members so the bus is over crowded and unsafe but we have no choice. The first children to be picked up are on the bus for over 2 hours before they reach the project and the same in the afternoon on their way home. Often this causes the children to overheat and become uncomfortable and dehydrated. 

Due to the current location of the project some of the children live on one side of the river Nile and the others live on the same side as the project. If we had 2 vehicles we could split the round into 2 and have 1 bus collect the children from across the river whilst the other bus collects the children from our side of the river. This would mean that the children spent less time on the bus, that neither bus would be overcrowded and that the day service could start earlier than it does at present. 



Inside the bus is cramped and hot which is almost unbearable for the children in the heat

This bus is good for doing half of the round but we need a new bus to service the second half of the round



A second bus

A second hand bus ranges in price from $9,000 - $14,000 online. This is a major priority for us for a couple of reasons. Firstly we want the children to travel safely and in comfort and secondly we want to be able to expand the project and help more children. With a second bus we could also access the community on a regular basis as our current bus is often being used for other purposes during the day such as escorting children to clinics for health checks and taking members of the management team to meetings or government offices.
